Abstraction Cellular Automata

I implemented automated composition with abstraction. By abstraction, I mean that the player does not have to worry about what is going on with the webAudio components. Factors that determine some components such as pitch and speed are hidden to the users, and they are unable to manually set it. By playing the game, they choose what sound they are playing.

Using "https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/npm/p5@1.1.9/lib/p5.js", I created cellular automata- Game of Life. One can click several cells on a 2d matrix and click play to generate sound. The sound changes as the cells live/die.

Rule of the game:

    -A live cell with 2 or 3 live neighbors survives to the next round.
    -A live cell with 4 or more neighbors dies of overpopulation.
    -A live cell with 1 or 0 neighbors dies of isolation.
    -An empty cell with exactly 3 neighbors becomes a live cell in the next round.


Specifications of the sound effect:

    -Waveform: When a cell reaches a wall, a specific waveform is added to list of potential waveform to be set.
    -FM Synthesis: change pitch by altering modulator frequency and gain value- they are proportional to current alive cells.
    -Speed: with alive/total cell ratio, change both the speed of sound and game.
    -Randomness: using row as i and column as j, when (i+j)%3 equals to 0 or 1, play an oscillator with specific frequency determined by i and j.
    -Game over: when all cells die, a specific sound created with additive synthesis is played. Using setTargetAtTime(0, audioCtx.currentTime, 0.8), create dramatic, long lasting sound effect


In addition, I added an oscilloscope using webAudio analyser. Setting its FFT frequency domain as 512, so that the graph is not too squished. Player can view the visual of the sound that the game is creating at the moment they click the "view oscilloscope" button, as the game pauses and same sound effect is played when viewing the oscilloscope.

Combining visual components with sound was very satisfying and interesting- rather than letting sound control the visuals like we did in class, in this project my initial selection of cells affected the sound output.
